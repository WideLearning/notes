# product measure
From [[measure theory]]
$\physics$
## Definition
Consider [[measure space|measure spaces]] $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathcal{B}, \nu)$. For $A \in \mathcal{A}, B \in \mathcal{B}$ we call $A \times B$ a measurable rectangle.
Then we define a [[premeasure]] $\lambda(A \times B) = \mu(A) \times \nu(B)$ and find its [[Caratheodory measure]] $\lambda^{*}$.

## Properties
- If $A \times B = \bigsqcup A_{k} \times B_{k}$, $\mu(A) \times \nu(B) = \sum\limits \mu(A_{k}) \times \nu(B_{k})$
- Product measure $\lambda^{*}$ can be expressed via iterated integration.
